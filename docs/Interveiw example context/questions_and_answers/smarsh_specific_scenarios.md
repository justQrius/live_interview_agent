## 7. A Compliance Officer Is Drowning in False Alerts

### Interviewer Intent (Why This Is a Core Smarsh Question)

This scenario tests whether you understand compliance *as lived reality*, not as a feature checklist.

Dominic is listening for whether you:
- Understand alert fatigue as an operational risk
- Can position AI as noise reduction, not magic
- Respect the human analyst's role in regulated workflows

### Core Framing (Open With Empathy)

"False positives aren't just annoying — they're dangerous, because they train teams to ignore real risk."

That line shows you understand the problem at a human and organizational level.

### Canonical Answer (Smarsh-Aligned)

"I'd start by reframing the problem with the compliance officer. The goal isn't 'more alerts' — it's *better signal per analyst hour*.

First, I'd assess where the noise is coming from: legacy keyword lexicons, lack of context, or inconsistent data capture across channels. In many environments, the system is doing exactly what it was configured to do — it just isn't aligned with intent.

From there, I'd walk them through how Smarsh reduces noise by adding context: understanding who is speaking, in what role, on which channel, and how that behavior compares to baseline patterns.

Practically, that means fewer alerts triggered by harmless language and more focus on messages that show risky intent or anomalous behavior.

Just as importantly, I'd emphasize human-in-the-loop workflows. AI should triage and prioritize, not replace judgment. The outcome we aim for is analysts spending time on high-probability risk instead of clearing thousands of false positives."

### Why This Answer Lands

You are:
- Validating the compliance officer's pain
- Positioning AI as an operational efficiency tool
- Avoiding overpromising or 'black box' narratives

This aligns perfectly with Smarsh's Communications Intelligence posture.

### 30-Second Version

"I frame false positives as an operational risk. The solution isn't more rules — it's more context. Smarsh uses AI to reduce noise by understanding intent and behavior, while keeping humans in control of final decisions."

### If Dominic Pushes: 'How Do You Prove This in a PoC?'

"I'd define success as measurable noise reduction — for example, fewer alerts per analyst per day — and validate that with real customer data during the PoC. If we can't prove operational improvement, the PoC isn't successful."

That shows discipline and credibility.

### Mistakes You Avoid

- Claiming AI 'solves compliance'
- Dismissing lexicons outright
- Ignoring analyst trust and adoption

## Additional Smarsh-Specific Scenarios

### Scenario: Unresponsive Stakeholders During a PoC

**Approach**:

1. **Diagnose**: Is it lack of time, lack of priority, or technical blockers?
   - Call IT lead directly: "I know you're busy—what can we do to make this easier for you?"

2. **Escalate Through AE**: Ask AE to engage customer's executive sponsor to unblock

3. **Offer More Support**: "We can handle more of the setup ourselves if you give us access."

4. **Set Regular Touchpoints**: "Let's do 15-min daily standups to keep momentum."

5. **Adjust POC Scope**: If they're genuinely overloaded, reduce POC scope to essentials

6. **Document Impact**: If delays persist, document timeline slippage and propose revised schedule

**Key Message**: Proactive, empathetic, solutions-focused (not complaining to AE)

---

### Scenario: Customer Uses Microsoft Purview—Why Switch to Smarsh?

**Approach**:

1. **Acknowledge Strength**: "Purview is excellent for the Microsoft 365 ecosystem."

2. **Discovery Questions**:
   - "What percentage of your communications happen outside M365?"
   - "Do traders use Bloomberg Terminal? WhatsApp? Salesforce?"

3. **Gap Identification**: "Purview can't capture Bloomberg, WhatsApp, or third-party systems—creating compliance blind spots."

4. **Independence Argument**: "Regulators prefer independent archives. Microsoft as both platform and archive creates potential conflict."

5. **Business Case**: "Smarsh provides single pane of glass for ALL communications vs. fragmented compliance view."

**Key Message**: Don't trash Microsoft, identify gaps through discovery, build business case

---

### Scenario: Handling POC Scope Creep

**Prevention Approach**:

"Define success criteria upfront in writing and get customer sign-off."

**When Scope Creeps Anyway**:

1. **Acknowledge**: "I understand you'd also like to see X. That's valuable."

2. **Negotiate**: "We can add X if we extend timeline by 1 week, OR we can note it for Phase 2."

3. **Refocus**: "Remember, our primary goal is validating Y and Z. Let's ensure we nail those first."

4. **Document**: Any scope changes documented and re-approved by both sides

**Key Message**: "I'm here to prove value, not to do infinite free consulting."
