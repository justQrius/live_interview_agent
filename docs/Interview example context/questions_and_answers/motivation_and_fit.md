## 10. Why Smarsh, Why Now

### Interviewer Intent (What This Really Tests)

This is not a passion question.

Dominic is evaluating whether your career trajectory *naturally converges* on Smarsh — or whether you're trying to talk yourself into the role.

He is listening for:
- Market timing awareness
- Product and domain understanding
- A credible reason *now* is the right moment

### Core Framing (Open With This)

"I've consistently gravitated toward environments where technology, regulation, and real-world consequences intersect — and Smarsh sits exactly at that intersection."

That sentence anchors everything.

### Canonical Answer (Structured, Conviction-Based)

"When I look at Smarsh, three things stand out.

First, the problem space. Regulated communications aren't getting simpler — volume is exploding, channels are fragmenting, and regulators are raising expectations. That's exactly the kind of complexity I've spent my career helping customers manage.

Second, the timing. The industry is at an inflection point where basic archiving is no longer enough. Customers need intelligence — but they need it delivered responsibly, with governance, explainability, and auditability built in. That aligns very closely with how I've been approaching AI: as a force multiplier for risk reduction, not experimentation.

Third, the role itself. Solutions Engineering at Smarsh isn't about flashy demos — it's about guiding institutions through high-stakes decisions. That blend of technical depth, stakeholder alignment, and trust-building is where I do my best work.

Taken together, Smarsh doesn't feel like a pivot for me — it feels like a continuation of the same trajectory, just at a larger scale and higher impact."

### 30-Second Version

"Smarsh sits at the intersection of regulation, scale, and AI — which is exactly where my career has been heading. The timing is right because customers need intelligence layered onto compliance, and that's the kind of high-stakes problem I'm motivated to solve."

### If Dominic Pushes: 'Why Not Somewhere Else?'

"Because few companies operate at this scale of regulatory consequence. Smarsh isn't selling convenience — it's selling defensibility. That's a harder problem, and a more meaningful one."

### Mistakes You Avoid

- Generic enthusiasm
- Over-indexing on AI buzzwords
- Treating Smarsh as a stepping stone

## What I Value in Sales–Presales Partnerships

### Interviewer Intent

Testing cultural fit, collaboration mindset, and maturity in enterprise sales environments.

### Strong Answer

"**Trust and Transparency**: AEs trust me to handle technical side; I trust them on commercial. No surprises—if I see a deal at risk technically, I tell them early.

**Shared Goals**: We win together or lose together. I don't blame AEs for 'bad leads' and they don't blame me for 'bad demos.'

**Complementary Strengths**: I bring technical depth and consultative approach; they bring business acumen and closing skills. When we play to our strengths, we're unstoppable.

**Continuous Feedback**: I ask AEs: 'What could I have done better in that demo?' and I give them product feedback too.

**Example**: Best AE partnerships I've had involved weekly strategy sessions, joint customer research, and celebrating wins together."

**Key Message**: Collaborative, mature, team-oriented

## What I'm Looking for in My Next Role

### Strategic Answer Framework

"Three things matter most to me:

**1. Impact at Scale**: I want to work on problems that matter—where getting it right protects institutions and where getting it wrong has real consequences. Smarsh operates at that level.

**2. Technical Evolution**: I want to be somewhere the technology is evolving fast enough to stay interesting—AI, cloud-native architectures, data at scale—but with discipline around governance and reliability, not just experimentation.

**3. Team and Culture**: I want to work with people who value both depth and outcomes—technical excellence without losing sight of business impact. From what I've seen of Smarsh and this team, that balance exists here."

**Key Message**: Thoughtful, specific, aligned with Smarsh's strengths

## Questions to Ask Dominic

### Strategic Questions (Show Long-Term Thinking)

**1. Team Vision**:
"Given your experience building presales teams at Salesforce and Pega, what's your vision for the Smarsh SE organization over the next 2 years?"
- Shows interest in team building, long-term thinking

**2. Product Strategy**:
"How is the GenAI roadmap changing the SE's role in demos and POCs?"
- Demonstrates awareness of AI trends, forward-thinking

**3. Success Metrics**:
"What does 'great' look like for a Senior SE in the first 90 days?"
- Shows results orientation, desire for clarity

### Role-Specific Questions

**4. Deal Complexity**:
"What's the typical deal size and sales cycle length for the accounts this SE will support?"
- Practical understanding of role scope

**5. Collaboration Model**:
"How do SEs collaborate with Product teams when customers request features on roadmap?"
- Shows interest in product influence, feedback loops

**6. Presales Challenges**:
"What's the biggest challenge you're tackling in scaling the presales team right now?"
- Demonstrates awareness that he's in "build mode"
