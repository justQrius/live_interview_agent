### Regulatory Landscape (2025-2026 Priorities)

**Priority 1: Anti-Money Laundering (AML), Fraud, and Sanctions**
- FINRA deficiencies in CIP (Customer Identification Programs), CDD (Customer Due Diligence), SAR (Suspicious Activity Reporting)
- June 2025: Expansion of U.S. sanctions against cryptocurrency shadow banking (72-hour compliance requirement)
- Demonstrates surgical precision and speed of modern regulatory response

**Priority 2: AI Governance**
- SEC requires firms to have adequate AI policies for fraud prevention, back-office ops, AML, trading
- 2025 GAO Report: "Insufficient explainability in AI models inhibits understanding, independent review, and compliance"
- Competitive advantage for Smarsh's domain-trained, transparent AI vs. black-box systems

**Priority 3: Third-Party Risk Management**
- FINRA's first-ever section on third-party risk (2025 Report)
- Firms must establish supervisory systems for vendor compliance
- Due diligence, data protection validation, comprehensive vendor lists required

**Priority 4: Investment Advisers as Financial Institutions**
- Starting January 1, 2026: SEC-registered investment advisers (RIAs) and exempt reporting advisers (ERAs) classified as "financial institutions" under Bank Secrecy Act
- New requirements: Full AML/CFT programs, SAR filing, travel rule compliance
- Penalties: Up to $5,000 per violation
- **Implication for Smarsh**: Expands TAM to entire RIA market segment

### Sales Cycle & POC Best Practices

**Typical Enterprise SaaS Sales Cycles**:
- Duration: 6-12+ months (longer for compliance tech due to regulatory validation)
- Stakeholders: Average of 5 key decision-makers
- CFO approval: 79% of enterprise deals require final CFO sign-off

**POC Best Practices** (Evidence-Based):
- Timeline: 7-21 days (longer risks momentum loss)
- Early alignment on success criteria before POC starts
- Weekly stakeholder engagement to maintain momentum
- Real or high-fidelity representative data
- Final structured review against success criteria → explicit buy/no-buy decision

**Smarsh-Specific POC Complexity**:
1. Integration setup (email, Teams, voice, mobile systems)
2. Data ingestion (representative/real samples)
3. Configuration (AI models, surveillance rules tuned to customer risk profile)
4. User training (Smarsh workflows)
5. Results measurement (false positive reduction, faster review, cost savings, risk detection)

### MEDDIC/MEDDPICC - Detailed Application for Smarsh

**Metrics (M)**:
- % reduction in false positives
- Faster review time per communication
- Cost savings from eliminated legacy systems
- Reduced regulatory examination findings
- Employee productivity improvements
- **SE Role**: Help prospect articulate metrics aligned to Smarsh's 95% false positive reduction capability

**Economic Buyer (E)**:
- Typically: CFO, Chief Compliance Officer (CCO), Chief Risk Officer (CRO)
- **SE Role**: Understand economic buyer's priorities (cost reduction, risk mitigation, operational efficiency); communicate ROI in business terms

**Decision Criteria (D)**:
- Technical: System scalability, channel integration, API flexibility
- Operational: Ease of use, deployment timeline, support quality
- Strategic: Vendor viability, roadmap, partnership model
- Financial: Pricing, licensing model, total cost of ownership
- **SE Role**: Position Smarsh strengths early (e.g., "100+ channels vs. competitor's 60")

**Decision Process (DP)**:
1. Needs assessment/RFI
2. Vendor evaluation/demos
3. POC
4. Competitive evaluation
5. Legal/security review
6. CFO sign-off
7. Contract negotiation
8. Implementation planning
- **SE Role**: Map process, identify bottlenecks (e.g., security review delays), plan SE engagement at each stage

**Identify Pain (I)**:
- Recent regulatory examination findings
- Pending audits
- Remote work supervision gaps
- Legacy system technical debt
- Employee productivity loss from manual review
- **SE Role**: Probe beneath surface to understand root pain and quantify business impact

**Champion (C)**:
- Typically: Technical/compliance staff who recognize value
- **SE Role**: Identify early, involve in POCs, arm with data to persuade stakeholders

**Paper Process (P)**:
- Legal review, security assessment, vendor onboarding, compliance sign-offs, procurement negotiations
- **SE Role**: Proactively surface delays (e.g., SOC 2, DPA requirements), propose solutions

**Competition (C)**:
- Incumbent vendors, DIY solutions, status quo, alternative budget priorities
- **SE Role**: Differentiate based on petabyte-scale AI, cloud-native architecture
